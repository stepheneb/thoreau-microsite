Thoreau’s snowshoes
On a trip to Maine,
Thoreau bought snowshoes
made by the Penobscot people.
This purchase prompted him
to study whether winters
had become less snowy in
Concord over time.
This site works best with sound.
Perhaps the best evidence of an amelioration of the climate—is the snow-shoes which lie about in so many garrets—now useless…
Henry David Thoreau
